## Scene 1 — Dusk at the Well

The well is stone, lip-high to you, the particular grey that stone turns when the light stops trying. The village is behind you. The path is behind you. The evening is arriving the way evenings arrive here — not suddenly, but as a weight.

Three villagers are already at the well. Mara, who is an elder, is standing a few paces back. She has a jar in her hand. It is empty. Iven is closer to the low wall, facing the well but not reaching toward it. The third one — you still confuse him with his brother; the quiet one, the one who stands near paths — is almost on the path itself, as if he has either just arrived or is about to leave.

None of them are drawing water.

You came here to fill your jar. You are going to fill your jar.

---

You walk to the lip of the well. You set your jar down on the stone. You reach over and touch the surface of the water with your fingers, to test for warmth before lowering the jar.

*`water_touch` fires — passive Sight event. No roll.*

The water is warm.

You say it aloud before you know you have said it.

*"The water is warm."*

It is not sun-warm — the sun is almost gone. It is not stone-warm — the stone is cold under your other hand. It is warm the way something is warm when it has been held. When it has been near a body. You think of putting your hand into your pocket and finding another hand already there, warm, and the hand is yours, but it is also not.

You pull your fingers out. You do not remember deciding to pull them out.

Mara has taken a step closer. Not toward the well — toward you. Iven's mouth is open. He is not speaking. The one on the path has not moved.

The village is watching you.
